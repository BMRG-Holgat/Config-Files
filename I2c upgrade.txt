# 📘 I²C OVER CAT5 – PRACTICAL DESIGN GUIDE (FOR YOUR SYSTEM)

## 🎯 Goal

Run multiple I²C device clusters (MCP23017, PCA9685, OLED) over Cat5 using a mux, with stable operation and no bus failures.

---

# ⚠️ CORE PROBLEM (WHY YOUR CURRENT DESIGN FAILS)

Your current architecture uses:

* P82B715DR in **daisy chains**
* Multiple pull-up domains
* Mixed I²C devices on long Cat5 runs

### Result:

* Bus capacitance overload
* Signal distortion
* MUX channel selection failures (`status=2`)
* Intermittent or stuck I²C buses

---

# ❌ WHAT NOT TO DO

### 🚫 Do NOT:

* Chain multiple P82B715 extenders
* Run raw I²C directly over Cat5 without buffering
* Mix multiple pull-up networks per cable
* Treat I²C like Ethernet (it is not packet-based)

---

# 🧩 BEST HARDWARE APPROACH

## 🥇 OPTION 1 (RECOMMENDED): Proper I²C over Cat5

Use:

* TCA9617A

### Rules:

* ONE buffer per Cat5 cable
* NO chaining buffers
* Each branch is independent

### Wiring:

```text
MUX → TCA9617A → Cat5 → device cluster
```

---

## 🥈 OPTION 2: Differential Cat5 (robust but more complex)

Use:

* PCA9615

### When to use:

* Long cables (>2–5m)
* High noise environments
* Industrial setups

### Wiring:

```text
MUX → PCA9615 → Cat5 → PCA9615 → cluster
```
